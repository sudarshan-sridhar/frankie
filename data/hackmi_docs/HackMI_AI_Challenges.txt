CHALLENGE 01  ·  DTE ENERGY
01 / 03
Utility Pole Risk Profiling
Moving from reactive pole maintenance to a predictive, risk-based approach.
THE PROBLEM
Utility poles hold up the entire electric and communications grid, yet traditional inspection cycles miss early warning signs and can't prioritize the highest-risk areas. With public datasets — weather history, satellite imagery, vegetation indices, flood maps — and modern ML and GIS tools, there's an opportunity to score risk proactively instead of reactively.
WHAT TEAMS WILL BUILD
Aggregate weather, imagery, vegetation, soil & geo datasets
Model pole and circuit context with proxy assumptions where data is thin
Generate explainable risk scores by pole or circuit segment
Visualize on a map-based dashboard with filtering and prioritization
Public
data, open tools, creative modeling — no proprietary inputs required.
THE QUESTION
How can accessible data and modern tools move utility maintenance from reactive to predictive — improving reliability and optimizing limited resources?

CHALLENGE 02  ·  STARTMIDWEST
02 / 03
Innovation Ecosystem Discovery
Closing the visibility gap that drives Midwest founders to the coasts.
THE PROBLEM
Founders struggle to find grants, incentives, accelerators, and events because the data is fragmented across city and state portals — and decays the moment it's published. StartMidwest wants a dual-sided AI platform: a conversational discovery tool for founders, and an operator layer that detects changes, ingests new sources, and routes them through human-in-the-loop verification.
WHAT TEAMS WILL BUILD
Conversational discovery interface with actionable next steps
Change detection that diffs sources and surfaces meaningful updates
Review queue for human curators to approve, edit, or reject AI proposals
State-agnostic architecture — Michigan first, replicable to other states
~100
Michigan resources in the baseline dataset — over a year old, partly stale, and needing a system that keeps itself fresh.
THE QUESTION
When a real founder uses your tool, do they get to the moment where they say 'wait, that exists — how have I never heard of this?'

CHALLENGE 03  ·  THE AI COLLECTIVE DETROIT
03 / 03
Michigan Small Business AI Adoption
AI access equity for Michigan main streets — built for owners without a tech team.
THE PROBLEM
Most AI tooling is built for companies that already have engineers. Michigan small business owners — restaurants, salons, contractors, neighborhood shops — don't have that team. Aligned with TechTown, Bamboo, and the existing partner network, this challenge is about hitting the access equity piece directly: build something a Michigan small business owner without a tech team can actually deploy.
WHAT TEAMS WILL BUILD
Solves a real, repeated pain point for a specific small business type
Deployable in 30 days with no engineering staff on payroll
Total operating cost under $50/month using off-the-shelf tooling
Owner-friendly onboarding — usable on day one, not after training
<$50
per month, zero engineering staff, deployable in 30 days — the entire constraint surface.
THE QUESTION
If a Michigan small business owner with no tech team finds your tool tomorrow, can they have it running — and earning back its cost — within a month?
