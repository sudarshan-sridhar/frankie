The hackathon challenge
In this hackathon, you will build an AI-driven solution using IBM Bob for the following theme:
Make work less boring
This is your chance to show the world what “different” could look like when it comes to business. Use IBM Bob to build a proof-of-concept, AI-driven solution that brings creativity into how businesses operate. Whether it’s generating new ideas, designing creative content, predicting innovation or market trends, enhancing brainstorming processes, or nurturing creative strategy — you’ll identify an activity that can be improved and build a solution that empowers businesses to innovate faster and more creatively.
Refer to the Make work less boring example use cases in the Appendix.
A note on data sets before you begin
Participants are required to bring their own datasets to build the solution aligning to your use case. As you collect data for your project, you’ll want to use best practices. Here are helpful tips:
Teams are responsible for ensuring data is compliant.
Data from public websites may be used, if the terms allow for commercial use, but please keep a list of the websites you use.
Do not use data or assets containing company confidential data, or any other data without permission from the data owner. Teams are responsible for getting approval.
Do not use any client data.
Do not use any data containing personal information (PI).
Do not use data obtained from social media.
Get started with IBM Bob
IBM Bob is the primary AI tool for Hack Michigan. This section covers how to request your free trial, what your Bobcoin allocation looks like, and how to access your admin portal during the event.
Free trial plan and Bobcoin limitation
Each interaction with Bob’s AI capabilities consumes Bobcoins. The free trial plan comes with a limitation of 40 Bobcoins for 30 days. When Bobcoins are exhausted or the 30 days end, the account becomes inactive unless upgraded to a paid plan.
Plan ahead: With only 40 Bobcoins available across 30 days, plan your Bob usage strategically for the 48 hours of the hackathon. Use Bob for high-impact moments — drafting key content, exploring an idea, or testing a workflow — rather than every small task. Coordinate within your team on who is signed in so you don’t accidentally double-spend Bobcoins on the same prompts.
Step 1 — Subscribe for the trial plan
Open the Bob register for trial URL in your system browser.
Create an IBM account by filling out the form and selecting the Next button. If you already have an existing IBM account, you can proceed to log in and complete the authentication.
Verify your email by entering the 7-digit code sent to your email and check the optional “IBM keeping you informed on products and services” box. Select the Submit button.
An IBM and Bob account will be created and you will get a confirmation that your trial account is ready.
You will also receive an email confirmation for subscribing to the trial plan.
Step 2 — Get started with your free trial and access the admin portal
After successfully subscribing for the trial plan, the Bob team will take about 5 minutes to get your account ready. Once it is ready, you will receive an email confirmation, and then you can download and get started using Bob.
Access your Bob admin portal to track your Bob usage and see account-related information.
Follow the instructions below to access your admin portal:
Log in to your IBM Bob account using your free trial subscription email.
Access the admin portal. If you have multiple Bob accounts, please ensure you select the correct instance.
You will see your trial subscription plan details, including remaining Bobcoins and days left on your trial.
Tips for working efficiently with Bob during the hackathon
Front-load your highest-leverage prompts. Use early Bobcoins to validate your idea and shape your approach before you’re deep into building.
Track your Bobcoin usage in the admin portal so you don’t run out at a critical moment in the build.
Coordinate within your team on who is signed in to Bob, so you don’t accidentally double-spend Bobcoins on duplicate prompts.
Save useful prompts and outputs locally — your Bob trial expires after 30 days, so keep a record of what worked for your final submission and write-up.

Option 2: IBM watsonx.ai
Teams that want to use the watsonx.ai SaaS runtime to inference Granite models can request access through the hackathon site. Once registered, follow the “Complete the hackathon” page instructions to request an IBM Cloud account.
watsonx.ai credit usage: Each provisioned account receives $100 in credits. Foundation model inferencing consumes tokens (1,000 tokens = 1 RU at $0.0001 USD), and notebook runtimes are billed per Capacity Unit Hour (CUH) at $1.02 USD/CUH. Email alerts are sent at 25%, 50%, and 80% usage; accounts are deactivated within an hour at 100%. Plan accordingly and back up your work.
Out of scope features: Agent Studio (Beta), Deploy on IBM Cloud/watsonx.ai, Bring your own model, Fine-tuning, and Cloud Object Storage are not available for this hackathon.
Access your IBM Cloud account
Once provisioned, all team members will receive an email invite to join the cloud account. Follow these steps:
Check the email inbox you used to register for the hackathon and open the email from the IBM Cloud team.
Click the Join Now button in the email. A new browser tab will open with the cloud account sign-up page.
Review your account and personal information.
Click the Join now link in the email to begin joining the cloud account.
Read and accept the Account notice and click the Join Account button.
Complete the authentication process by clicking the Continue button.
After you authenticate successfully, you will be taken to the IBM Cloud dashboard.
Important note
If you have an existing personal IBM Cloud account on the same email/IBMid, you may sometimes be directed to your personal account. In this case, switch to the xxxxxxx – watsonx account from the account drop-down at the top-right of the dashboard.
Access Prompt Lab on watsonx.ai
After successfully joining the IBM Cloud account, you can access the Prompt Lab on the watsonx.ai platform to work with the Granite models supported on the platform.
Log in at https://dataplatform.cloud.ibm.com/wx/home?context=wx with the email you used for IBM Cloud.
Confirm the account name is xxxxxxx – watsonx and the region is set to Dallas.
Click Open Prompt Lab on the “Chat and build prompts with foundational models” widget.
The Prompt Lab editor opens with a chat window to get you started.
Selecting an IBM Granite model
A granite-3-8b-instruct model is pre-selected. To switch:
Open the AI Model drop-down and click View all foundation models.
Clear all filters and search “granite”. All Granite series models will be displayed.
See choosing a model for guidance on modalities, supported languages, tuning, and indemnification.
Programmatic access (API endpoint)
To call watsonx.ai APIs/SDKs, you’ll need three values: a project ID, endpoint URL, and API key.
On the watsonx.ai home page, scroll down to the Developer access section.
Select Project or space → watsonx Challenge Sandbox. A project ID will be displayed.
A default watsonx.ai endpoint URL will be displayed for the Dallas region.
Click Create API key, enter a name, choose “Disable the leaked key”, and click Create.
Copy and store the API key securely — you can also download the file.
Access the prompt code from the Prompt Lab editor
To prompt an AI model programmatically, click the View code icon at the top-right of the Prompt Lab editor.
The prompt code is available as cURL, Node.js, and Python.
To authorize requests, replace the ${YOUR_ACCESS_TOKEN} placeholder with an IAM access token generated from your API key.
Generating an IAM access token
POST to https://iam.cloud.ibm.com/identity/token with header Content-Type: application/x-www-form-urlencoded and body grant_type=urn:ibm:params:oauth:grant-type:apikey&apikey=YOUR_API_KEY. IAM tokens are valid for up to 60 minutes — check the expires_in property and refresh as needed.

Saving your Prompt Lab session
You can save your Prompt Lab editor session for later use.
At the top of the Prompt Lab screen, select the Save work dropdown and choose Save as.
In the Save your work widget, select Prompt session under Asset type, enter a name, check View in project after saving, and click Save.
You can also save your work as a Prompt template (current prompt only) or Notebook (Jupyter Notebook environment, requires Python knowledge).
Saving your work on watsonx.ai
IBM Cloud accounts are deactivated at the end of the hackathon. To export your project:
Go to your project’s ‘Overview’ tab.
Click the ‘Export or import project’ drop-down beneath the Bell icon and select ‘Export project’.
Select all assets (project sessions cannot be exported), click ‘Export’, confirm sensitive information has been removed, and click ‘Continue export’ to download the .zip.
